Andromeda Galaxy

Andromeda is the closest large galaxy to the Milky Way, and it is anticipated to collide with it in about 4.5 billion years. The two will eventually unite to form a single new galaxy called Milkdromeda.

Bode's Galaxy

Messier 81 is another name for it. The M81 Group's biggest galaxy. A supermassive black hole with 70 million times the mass of the Sun is located there.

Condor Galaxy

With a diameter of more than 665,300 light-years (204.0 kiloparsecs), it is the biggest spiral galaxy known to science. The tiny lenticular galaxy IC 4970 disturbs it tidally.
